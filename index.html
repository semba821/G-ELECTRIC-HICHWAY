G-ELECTRIC HIGHWAY

Transforming roads into sustainable energy sources

Introduction

We present a sustainable and innovative solution to the global energy crisis by converting mechanical energy from road traffic into electricity using Triboelectric Nanogenerators (TENGs). This advanced yet cost-effective technology captures everyday motion—such as vehicles and pedestrians on asphalt—and transforms it into clean, storable energy.

Every step, every wheel rotation becomes a source of renewable power.

About Us

We are a team of high school students passionate about generating clean energy from everyday materials. Our mission is to contribute to a more sustainable future by finding innovative ways to protect the environment and support the economy.

Our work is supported by recent studies published in scientific journals such as Nano Energy and Advanced Materials, and offers a practical model for real-world deployment in smart cities and dense urban environments.

The Technology

What is TENG?
Triboelectric Nanogenerators (TENGs) generate electricity through the triboelectric effect, occurring when two materials with differing electron affinity come into contact and separate, creating charge transfer and voltage.

  • Contact-Separation Mode (used in our project)
  • Sliding Mode
  • Single-Electrode Mode
  • Freestanding Mode

The Contact-Separation Mode is ideal under vertical pressure from road traffic.

Scientific Principle:

Q = σ × A

  • Q = Generated charge
  • σ = Surface charge density
  • A = Contact area

A higher contact area and greater material difference result in more electric output.

System Design

  • Two triboelectric layers with opposite charge behavior
  • Conductive layer for charge collection
  • Flexible support structure placed beneath asphalt
  • Environmental protection layers
  • Energy storage units such as supercapacitors or lithium-ion batteries

How It Works:

  1. A vehicle or pedestrian creates mechanical pressure.
  2. Contact and separation generate electric charge.
  3. The charge transfers via the conductive layer.
  4. The energy is stored or fed into a system.

Materials Used

Positive Layer: Sheep Wool

  • High triboelectric position
  • Natural and biodegradable
  • High surface roughness increases charge

Negative Layer: Recycled Polypropylene

  • Strong electron affinity
  • Low-cost and durable

Conductive Layer: Aluminum Foil

  • Excellent conductivity
  • Lightweight and corrosion-resistant

Additional Components:

  • Zinc Oxide (ZnO)
  • Graphite
  • Mineral Sand
  • Conductive Cement

Applications

  • High-traffic roadways
  • Smart lighting systems
  • Traffic monitoring networks
  • Pedestrian walkways
  • Emergency energy sources
  • Hybrid solar-TENG systems

Impact and Performance

  • 1–10 milliwatts per unit from human motion
  • 100+ watts/m² in scaled systems

    • Enable self-powered IoT systems
